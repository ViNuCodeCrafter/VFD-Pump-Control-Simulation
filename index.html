Project Mission

This intelligent VFD (Variable Frequency Drive) controller uses Random Forest Regression to optimize water delivery in multi-floor residential systems. By analyzing real-time occupancy and tank levels, it dynamically adjusts pump speed to maintain constant pressure while minimizing energy consumption.

Technical Working Principle
1

Data Acquisition & Encoding

The system receives inputs from pressure transducers, ultrasonic tank sensors, and flow meters. Categorical data like Time Slot is transformed using One-Hot Encoding to allow the mathematical model to interpret cyclical usage patterns (e.g., peak morning usage).

2

The Random Forest Brain

Instead of a linear equation, our AI uses an **Ensemble of Decision Trees**. Each tree makes a prediction based on a random subset of features (Floor No, People Count, etc.). The forest averages these predictions to calculate the Target Pressure with extreme robustness against sensor noise.

3

Pump Simulation Feedback

The predicted commands are passed to a high-fidelity Pump Simulation. This digital twin calculates the motor's Hz response and simulates the resulting hydraulic pressure, allowing us to validate logic and predict up to 40% energy savings compared to traditional fixed-speed systems.
